About Me

My journey into Software Engineering

  • 1995-2006

    Learning to Dedicate

    Since I was young, I was always into sports. I played soccer and baseball religiously, that was, until I found football and never looked back. Sports taught me the discipline to achieve my goals, and I've carried that with me through all of my endeavours.

  • 2006-2012

    Creative Beginnings

    Once I got into high school, I started playing guitar and my eyes were opened to the infinite creative possibilities available. I use this in my front end development as an opportunity to try new things and create something beautiful

  • 2012-2015

    UCLA & First Contact

    When I got to UCLA, I was exposed to true computer science for the first time. Despite my interest, I wanted to dedicate my self to doing the best at my degree while I was there. I knew I would be back for it soon.

  • March 2015

    Post-Grad + Beginning

    In my time after college, I began coding as way to stay intellectually stimulated. I quickly learned how satisfying it was both intellectually and creatively and couldn't quench my thirst to learn more. It was then I knew I wanted to get serious about software engineering.

  • October 2015 - January 2016

    Coding Dojo Bootcamp

    After extensive research and self-learning for months, I joined the Coding Dojo to get my web and mobile development skills to a new level. Three months and multiple projects later, I achieved their double Black Belt certification.

  • Now

    React and new opportunities

    These days I'm learning React, React-Native, SASS, Rails, and looking for new ways to utilize these frameworks in fun and interesting ways. When I'm not building apps or learning new technology, I love playing bass in my band Rubicon, finding new places to hike, making home-made beer, and working out.
