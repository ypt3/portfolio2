About Me

My Software Engineering journey

  • 2004

    American Dream

    Came to United States when I was 15 and became diverse with the different cultures exposure which is key to how I can adapt easily to different environments.

  • 2008-2013

    B.S Electrical Engineering

    Once I got into college, I decided to pursue Electrical Engineering and learn practical experience for being a Hardware Engineer.

  • 2013-2015

    Hardware Engineer

    After college, I moved to the Silicon Valley to pursue a career in hardware. During my time there, I worked in QA as well as Product Manager handling over $2 milllion dollars worth of supplies (Switching Power supplies, Battery chargers, Power LED drivers, Switching servers, and more) at Phihong USA.

  • September 2015

    Software journey begins

    With my free time after work, I began coding as habit. I quickly learned how satisfying it was both intellectually and creatively and couldn't quench my thirst to learn more. Later, I realized how much I wanted to get serious about becoming a Software Engineer.

  • October 2015 - February 2016

    Coding Dojo Bootcamp

    After self-learning for months, I joined the bootcamp Coding Dojo to learn more in depth on my web development skills and hopefully, I can bring it to the next level. Fourteen weeks later, I was able to create multiple projects on my own and achieved their top certification of a double Black Belt.

  • Now

    Learn and new opportunities

    Now a days, I am learning new technology such as ReactJs, BackboneJs, SASS, and looking for new ways to utilize these frameworks in a fun and efficient way. A fun fact about me - when I am not building apps or learning new technology, I like to hang out with my friends, listen to EDM music, hiking, and working out.
